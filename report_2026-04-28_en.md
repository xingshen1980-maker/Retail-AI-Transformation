## c) In-Depth Case Analysis

### Case 1: Walmart — Sparky Agentic Commerce Engine
**Case Highlight:** Walmart's Sparky is not a chatbot — it is a multi-step agentic AI engine deployed across the Walmart mobile app, ChatGPT (March 2026), and Google Gemini. Sparky interprets complex natural language queries (occasion-based, image-based, voice), builds shopping baskets, checks real-time inventory across Walmart's 5,000+ US fulfilment-capable stores, and executes purchases. CEO Furner describes Sparky as "connecting digital intent to fulfilment through forward-deployed inventory and 1.5 million associates."

**Technical Approach:** Retail-specific LLMs trained on Walmart's proprietary authenticated data (purchase history, inventory, loyalty profiles). Sparky is being built as a multimodal agent (text, image, audio, video). The ChatGPT integration uses OpenAI's Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP), maintaining Walmart-controlled checkout — avoiding conversion loss from third-party checkout layers (Walmart ended its OpenAI Instant Checkout pilot after observing sharply lower conversion rates).

**Business Value:** 35% higher average order value for Sparky users; 81% of shoppers use it for product validation pre-purchase; Walmart's retail media business grew 33% YoY; agentic advertising assistant Marty is being tested for B2B advertiser optimisation. Sparky is framed as a demand-capture and customer frequency tool — not merely a cost play.

**Critical Assessment:** Walmart's approach of maintaining proprietary checkout control while embedding Sparky into third-party AI platforms (ChatGPT, Gemini) is strategically superior to full third-party checkout delegation. This "bring your own checkout" model protects conversion economics while expanding reach. *Highly replicable lesson for IKEA.*

### Case 2: Amazon — Alexa+ Agentic Shopping Assistant at Scale
**Case Highlight:** Amazon's February 2026 nationwide US rollout of Alexa+ — now expanding to Spain (April 23) — represents the most scaled deployment of a generative AI shopping assistant globally. Alexa+ integrates with thousands of services (Expedia, Grubhub, Uber Eats, OpenTable, Yelp) and enables full transactional journeys: grocery list to Amazon Fresh cart, recipe discovery to meal planning, travel research to booking — all within a conversational interface.

**Technical Approach:** Powered by Anthropic's Claude (LLM), with agentic capabilities enabling multi-step task execution across APIs and services. Persistent memory and context continuity across Echo devices, mobile, and Alexa.com web platform. Amazon's 600 million+ Alexa-enabled devices provide a hardware moat unavailable to most competitors.

**Business Value:** Users shopping **3× more**, 2–3× more conversations, 5× more recipe requests versus original Alexa. Amazon CEO Jassy: "AI is not a standalone initiative — it's a multiplier. It will reshape every customer experience we offer and unlock entirely new ones." AWS AI revenue surpasses $15B run rate, validating that AI infrastructure investment is generating commercial return.

**Critical Assessment:** Amazon's greatest strength is end-to-end integration: Alexa+ does not merely recommend — it executes. However, its $200B CapEx commitment is unprecedented and carries execution risk if AI workload growth does not materialise at projected scale. The parallel to 1990s telecom infrastructure overbuilding is a legitimate concern flagged by analysts.

## d) Insights and Recommendations for IKEA Business

### Recommendation 1: Deploy an IKEA Agentic Commerce Agent — Now
Walmart's Sparky data is definitive: agentic AI shopping assistants produce **35% higher average order value** when users engage with them. IKEA's high-consideration purchase model (furniture, home furnishings) is uniquely suited for a conversational AI agent that handles room planning, product matching, availability checking, and checkout in a single session. **Action:** Commission a proof-of-concept Sparky-equivalent for IKEA.com and the IKEA app within the next development cycle, integrating with IKEA's real-time inventory across fulfilment hubs. Follow Walmart's model: maintain IKEA-controlled checkout — do not cede transaction ownership to third-party AI platforms.

### Recommendation 2: Integrate IKEA into Third-Party AI Platforms Defensively
Sephora's ChatGPT app launch and Walmart's Sparky-in-Gemini strategy both demonstrate that leading retailers are embedding themselves inside AI discovery interfaces as a defensive measure against being disintermediated. If consumers begin researching "bedroom furniture for small spaces" in ChatGPT or Gemini and IKEA is not a recommended result, a competitor will be. **Action:** Initiate partnerships with OpenAI (ChatGPT) and Google (Gemini) to deploy IKEA's product catalogue via Agentic Commerce Protocol, anchored to IKEA Family loyalty account authentication. Prioritise discovery and inspiration — not checkout handover.

### Recommendation 3: Adopt AI-Powered Trend-to-Product Compression for Assortment Planning
Walmart's proprietary "Trend-to-Product" AI compresses concept-to-shelf from 26 weeks to **6–8 weeks**. Sephora's AI trend forecasting shortens its trend response cycle by **30–40%**. For IKEA, where seasonal living trends (biophilic design, micro-living, hybrid work) drive home furnishing demand, this is a directly applicable capability. **Action:** Build or procure an AI trend-signal-to-assortment tool that ingests cultural and social data (Pinterest, TikTok, interior design publications), generates product concepts, and connects directly to IKEA's product development pipeline. Target a 50% reduction in concept-to-production lead time.

### Recommendation 4: Implement AI-Powered Digital Human Associates for IKEA Live Commerce
JD.com's 90% live-stream production cost reduction via AI digital humans is the most operationally disruptive data point of the week. IKEA's in-store experience model is difficult to replicate digitally — but AI-powered virtual home design consultants running 24/7 live sessions on IKEA's platforms, Douyin (China), and TikTok Shop could dramatically extend reach at low marginal cost. **Action:** Pilot AI digital human technology (multiple vendors available: HeyGen, D-ID, JD's JoyAI platform for China markets) for live commerce sessions on IKEA's Chinese digital channels. Measure conversion rates and basket sizes versus human-hosted sessions.

### Recommendation 5: Accelerate AI Workforce Reskilling with Certification Infrastructure
Walmart's commitment to AI-proficiency certification for **2.1 million employees** — combined with a proprietary AI platform for daily use — is not a PR exercise. It is a structural capability investment ensuring AI tools are actually used in stores and supply chains. IKEA's 220,000+ global co-workers are a competitive asset; without systematic reskilling, AI tools will underperform in deployment. **Action:** Develop an IKEA AI Proficiency Certification programme (modelled on Walmart's framework) covering: AI-assisted store inventory management, AI customer service tools, AI-powered room planning assistance, and data literacy. Deploy an internal AI assistant for co-workers before scaling customer-facing agents.

## e) Risks and Observations

### Risk 1: Third-Party AI Platform Disintermediation — HIGH
The central competitive risk articulated by both Walmart and Amazon this week: if third-party AI assistants (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) become the primary interface for product discovery, retailers risk becoming "commoditised infrastructure layers." Walmart ended its OpenAI Instant Checkout pilot after conversion rates fell sharply when checkout left its own environment. IKEA must establish its platform presence in these AI interfaces now — before the discovery layer is captured by competitors.

### Risk 2: Agentic AI Accuracy and Customer Trust — MEDIUM
Amazon's own VP acknowledged that Alexa+ misfires are documented across social media, and 3% of users who try it opt out. Walmart's Sparky is explicitly labelled "results may not fully be accurate." For IKEA — where incorrect product dimensions, incompatible item recommendations, or availability errors could result in costly delivery returns — AI accuracy in agentic shopping contexts requires rigorous guardrails, human-in-the-loop escalation, and robust returns/exchange policy integration before broad deployment.

### Risk 3: Regulatory Exposure — EU AI Act Compliance — MEDIUM
The EU AI Act (fully enforced 2026), 18 California AI laws, and the Colorado AI Act (effective February 2026) collectively impose transparency requirements, consumer notification obligations when AI influences decisions, and impact assessments for high-risk AI systems. IKEA operates extensively across EU markets. Any AI-based pricing, product recommendation, or workforce management tool must be assessed against these frameworks before deployment. Douyin's requirement for "AI" watermarks on AI-hosted streams is an early model for mandatory AI disclosure in commerce.

### Risk 4: AI Infrastructure Cost vs. ROI Timeline — WATCH
Amazon's $200 billion 2026 CapEx commitment (+50% YoY) and Alibaba's RMB 380 billion three-year AI budget both carry execution risk. Analysts draw explicit parallels to 1990s telecom overbuilding. For IKEA, the lesson is precision over scale: Walmart's own agentic AI strategy stresses highly specific, purpose-built tasks rather than generic automation. ROI on AI investment in retail typically materialises over 12–24 months and depends heavily on workforce adoption rates and data quality — not merely model deployment.
