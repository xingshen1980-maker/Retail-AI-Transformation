## c) In-Depth Case Analysis

### Case 1 — Walmart: Agentic Commerce Ecosystem (Open Platform Strategy)

**Case Highlight:** In 2026, Walmart executed a pivotal strategic shift — from AI as an internal optimization layer to AI as an **open, multi-platform commerce fabric**. By integrating Sparky with ChatGPT, Gemini, and the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP, co-developed with Google, Shopify, and Target), Walmart allows AI agents to mediate product discovery, comparison, checkout, and post-purchase support across external platforms while keeping customer identity, order history, and loyalty data within Walmart's own ecosystem.

**Technical Approach:** Four purpose-built "super agents" trained on Walmart's proprietary retail data. Sparky handles consumer shopping; Marty manages supplier and advertiser workflows. UCP enables standardized, open API connections between Walmart's catalog and external AI agents. Digital shelf labels (electronic price displays) across 5,200 stores feed real-time pricing data into the same ecosystem.

**Business Value:** This architecture directly addresses the risk that AI-native search (ChatGPT, Gemini) could disintermediate traditional retail. Walmart's Gemini checkout integration — which collapses the gap between "I want it" and "I have it," per incoming CEO John Furner — converts external AI discovery moments into Walmart transactions, rather than ceding that moment to Amazon or direct-to-brand competitors. With US eCommerce at 23% of net sales and advertising growing 37% globally, the agentic commerce strategy is already yielding measurable returns.

---

### Case 2 — Amazon: Project Kobe — The Cloud-Operated Supercenter

**Case Highlight:** Project Kobe reframes what a physical retail store is: a **software-defined fulfillment node** capable of serving in-store shoppers, curbside pickup, and same-day delivery from a single, cloud-managed facility. The AI assistant Frida automates category selection, demand forecasting, and shelf allocation — reducing reliance on human category managers and enabling decisions to scale across multiple locations simultaneously.

**Technical Approach:** AutoStore robotic grids handle ambient, high-density warehouse operations embedded in the back of each store. A custom AI model processes demand signals and space constraints to generate assortment recommendations. Amazon's "Regionalization 2.0" supply chain strategy feeds local demand data into Frida's models. An in-house system called Orbital (targeting ambient, chilled, and frozen goods) is approximately two years from launch and will further reduce perishable fulfillment costs.

**Business Value:** Amazon currently controls just 3% of the US grocery market versus Walmart's 21%. Project Kobe is Amazon's most credible attempt to close this gap by combining its e-commerce scale with a physical presence that rivals Walmart's footprint. The model also converts Amazon's cloud and robotics infrastructure — already proven in fulfillment centers — into a consumer-facing competitive advantage. The risk: fulfillment costs per item are projected 12% higher than Amazon's sub-same-day network, and the first full Kobe location (Orland Park, IL) does not open until late 2027.

---

### Case 3 — Alibaba: "World Model" AI Investment and Agentic B2B Commerce

**Case Highlight:** Alibaba's $290M investment in ShengShu (Vidu) signals a strategic evolution beyond text LLMs toward **world model AI** — systems trained on video and real-world physical scenarios, essential for robotics and embodied AI. Simultaneously, Alibaba.com's AI Mode turns its global B2B marketplace into an agentic commerce platform where buyers submit natural language queries and receive end-to-end sourcing, pricing, logistics, and payment execution — with no manual search required.

**Technical Approach:** AI Mode is powered by Accio, a multimodal engine that interprets unstructured inputs (text, images, documents) and maps them to supplier capabilities across Alibaba's marketplace. It integrates with Trade Assurance and secure payments, creating a closed-loop agentic transaction. ShengShu's Vidu platform generates video and simulates physical environments, providing a foundation for next-generation embodied AI and retail robotics. Alibaba's OpenClaw framework allows businesses to build custom AI agents on top of this infrastructure.

**Business Value:** Alibaba's ecosystem advantage — spanning e-commerce, logistics (Cainiao), cloud, and food delivery — means its agentic AI can execute every step from chatbot prompt to physical delivery without third-party dependencies. This is a structural moat that rivals like Tencent and ByteDance cannot easily replicate. The RMB 380 billion AI transformation has already delivered 12% improvement in advertising ROI on Taobao/Tmall and contributed to Singles Day 2025's record CNY 1.11 trillion GMV.

## d) Insights and Recommendations for IKEA Business

**01 — Accelerate Integration into AI-Native Discovery Channels**
Sephora's ChatGPT launch and Walmart's Gemini/ChatGPT integrations demonstrate that consumers increasingly begin purchase journeys inside large language model interfaces. IKEA should evaluate integrating the IKEA catalog — particularly high-consideration, configurable products such as kitchens, wardrobes, and living room systems — into ChatGPT's shopping plugin and Google Gemini's commerce layer. The IKEA Kreativ 3D tool and Kreativ kitchen configurator (already live in 17 countries) are strong candidates for this agentic extension. Prioritize markets where the IKEA app already has strong engagement and where Gemini/ChatGPT penetration is measurable.

**02 — Pilot AI-Powered In-Store Media Network Across High-Traffic Stores**
Dollar General's 12,000-store AI audio rollout demonstrates that AI-driven in-store media — combining POS data, localized messaging, and brand partner advertising — is now viable at scale even for discount operators. IKEA's high-dwell-time store format and strong loyalty data make it a superior candidate for AI-audio and digital signage networks. A pilot program across 10-15 high-footfall IKEA stores, integrating Family loyalty data with contextual audio and digital display content, could generate measurable advertising revenue while enhancing in-store experience.

**03 — Formalize an "Open Commerce Protocol" Readiness Assessment**
The Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP), co-developed by Google, Walmart, Shopify, and Target, is emerging as an open standard for AI-agent-to-retailer integration. IKEA's product catalog, availability data, and pricing intelligence must be structured as machine-readable assets — not just human-facing content — to remain discoverable by AI agents using vector-based semantic retrieval. Commission a structured data audit of IKEA's product feed quality across all markets, and assign a cross-functional team to evaluate UCP participation within 90 days.

**04 — Expand AI Logistics Capabilities Through Locus Platform Scaling**
Ingka Group's acquisition of Locus (AI-powered logistics management with advanced route optimization and real-time tracking) is a high-value differentiator. However, the competitive landscape is moving rapidly: Walmart's Sparky ecosystem, JD.com's logistics AI 2.0, and Amazon's Project Kobe all treat last-mile delivery as a core AI battleground. IKEA should accelerate the Locus platform's global deployment beyond the current pilot scope, prioritizing markets where home delivery penetration is growing fastest and where delivery experience most directly affects Net Promoter Scores.

**05 — Leverage the AI Literacy Program as a Workforce Differentiation Platform**
IKEA's ongoing initiative to train 70,000+ co-workers in AI literacy across 31 countries is a genuine strategic asset at a time when competitors are forcing AI tools onto unprepared workforces. The Billie chatbot's redeployment of 8,500 call center employees as remote interior design advisors — now generating 3.3% of total revenue with a target of 10% by 2028 — is a powerful proof point. IKEA should publish a clear internal roadmap linking AI literacy milestones to specific new service revenue targets, and ensure the program is resourced to scale to 160,000 co-workers in scope within the current planning cycle.

## e) Risks and Observations

**MEDIUM — Agentic Commerce Disintermediation Risk**
As AI agents increasingly mediate product discovery (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity), retailers not integrated into those flows risk becoming invisible at the moment of consumer intent. This risk is not hypothetical — it is structurally embedded in how Walmart, Sephora, and Amazon are already operating. IKEA's large-format store model and category complexity make this risk acute for considered-purchase categories. Mitigation requires structured data investment and proactive platform partnerships now, not after consumer behavior has shifted.

**HIGH — Tariff and Supply Chain Volatility Disrupting AI-Driven Forecasting Models**
Walmart has explicitly flagged tariff pressure on general merchandise sourcing. Amazon faces geopolitical exposure through its Haul/private-label China sourcing. For IKEA, with a globally distributed supply chain and sourcing concentrated in cost-sensitive regions, AI demand forecasting models trained on pre-tariff data may produce inaccurate inventory and pricing signals. IKEA's AI pricing intelligence platform should be urgently retrained to incorporate tariff-scenario modeling and supply chain disruption signals.

**WATCH — AI Talent Attrition at Scale**
Alibaba's loss of three senior Qwen division executives in early 2026, including division head Lin Junyang, highlights a systemic risk: AI talent is scarce globally, and even well-resourced companies cannot retain it through compensation alone. As IKEA builds out its Global AI Department & Lab and expands the Chief Data & Analytics Officer remit under Burce Gültekin, proactive retention programs — including visible AI product ownership, publication rights, and flexible research mandates — should be embedded into the talent strategy.

**WATCH — Gap Between AI Ambition and Measured ROI**
Forrester's 2026 US Retail Tech Forecast notes that US retailers will spend $113 billion on technology in 2026 (+6.6% YoY), yet most AI agents remain "assistive" rather than truly autonomous. The mandate for retail leaders is clear: ensure every AI investment demonstrates profitability impact, business resilience improvement, or measurable customer value — not incremental complexity. IKEA's CDO Parag Parekh's framing — "the 'why' must always lead the way" — is precisely correct and should be operationalized through a formal AI ROI measurement framework across all active deployments.
