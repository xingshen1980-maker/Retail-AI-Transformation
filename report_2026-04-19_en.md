## c) In-Depth Case Analysis

### Case 1: Walmart's Universal Commerce Protocol — Becoming the Default AI Shopping Layer

**Case Highlight:** In early 2026, Walmart transitioned from AI as an internal optimisation tool to AI as an open, multi-platform commerce fabric. By joining the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) — co-developed with Google, Shopify, and Target — and simultaneously enabling purchases directly within ChatGPT and Google Gemini, Walmart has effectively embedded itself as a default transactional endpoint wherever AI agents operate.

**Technical Approach:** UCP provides a standardised API layer connecting AI agents (including external LLMs) with retailers for end-to-end commerce flows: discovery, purchase, loyalty, and post-purchase. Walmart's Gemini integration provides checkout embedded within the Gemini interface itself — eliminating the platform-switching friction that traditionally disrupted conversion. Internally, Walmart deploys four super-agents (including Sparky for consumers and Marty for advertisers) built on proprietary retail data rather than generic LLMs.

**Business Value:** This architecture collapses the gap between AI-mediated intent and completed purchase. With 240–255 million customers shopping weekly and data from 10,500 stores worldwide, Walmart's proprietary data flywheel becomes the training and feedback loop that generic AI platforms cannot replicate. The strategic implication: retailers not integrated into agentic commerce flows risk disappearing at the moment of consumer intent — a structural threat as conversational AI becomes the primary shopping interface.

### Case 2: Amazon's Project Kobe — AI-Native Physical Retail at Supercenter Scale

**Case Highlight:** Amazon's "Project Kobe" supercenters — disclosed and updated as of April 8, 2026 — represent the most ambitious physical retail reinvention in the company's history. The format fuses a general merchandise supercenter (250,000 SKUs, nearly double a typical Walmart) with a fully automated fulfillment warehouse embedded in the same building, capable of servicing in-store shoppers, curbside pickup, and same-day delivery from a single facility.

**Technical Approach:** Each Kobe store allocates approximately 100,000 sq ft (half the building) to warehouse operations powered by the Proteus and Sparrow robotic systems, with next-generation "Orbital" automation — handling ambient, chilled, and frozen goods — planned for future sites. AI-driven tools govern assortment decisions (what each store should carry based on local demand signals). Blue Jay multi-arm robots, deployed in parallel at existing fulfillment centers, handle picking, stowing, and consolidation simultaneously. Project Eluna (agentic AI) provides real-time operational intelligence to store and logistics managers in natural language.

**Business Value:** Kobe's fulfillment integration enables each store to serve as both a revenue-generating retail surface and a cost-efficient last-mile hub — directly challenging Walmart's store-as-fulfillment-node advantage. At approximately 12% higher per-item fulfillment cost than sub-same-day networks, the format is not yet optimised, but represents a fundamental infrastructure bet on physical + digital convergence. Amazon's $200B CapEx in 2026 signals willingness to absorb near-term cost compression for long-term structural advantage.

### Case 3: Sephora × OpenAI — Conversational Commerce Meets Beauty Expertise at Scale

**Case Highlight:** Sephora's ChatGPT app launch marks the first time a major beauty retailer has natively embedded its loyalty ecosystem, product catalog, and personalised recommendations inside an external AI platform — with checkout integration planned. The deployment leverages Sephora's 80+ million member Beauty Insider database as a personalisation engine, turning conversational AI from a discovery tool into a full commerce channel.

**Technical Approach:** The ChatGPT app pulls from Beauty Insider profile data to surface personalised recommendations based on individual purchase history, preferences, and skin profile. Conversational prompts — natural language beauty questions — are routed through Sephora's catalog intelligence. Loyalty benefits (free shipping, samples, rewards) are surfaced within the conversation. Organic recommendations within ChatGPT are driven primarily by contextual relevance and user intent; a sponsored experience layer creates a new retail media channel within the AI interface itself.

**Business Value:** Sephora's AI trend forecasting has already shortened its trend-response cycle by 30–40%, enabling faster product launches and stronger first-week sales. By adding conversational commerce within ChatGPT, Sephora gains access to discovery moments that occur before a shopper even thinks of beauty — as users ask ChatGPT questions about skincare routines, ingredients, or gift ideas. For IKEA, this case demonstrates the potential to intercept home furnishing decisions at the research stage, before the customer reaches any brand's own platform.

## d) Insights and Recommendations for IKEA Business

**01 — Integrate IKEA Commerce into External AI Agents Without Delay**
Walmart and Sephora have demonstrated that the competitive window for early mover advantage in conversational commerce is closing rapidly. IKEA should immediately pursue API integration with ChatGPT (OpenAI shopping), Google Gemini, and UCP-compliant frameworks to enable product discovery, room planning advice, and checkout initiation directly within these platforms. The IKEA catalog — with its rich product attributes, dimensions, compatibility data, and assembly context — is exceptionally well-suited for AI-mediated discovery. Prioritise the home furnishing planning use case: intercepting queries such as "how to furnish a small living room" with shoppable IKEA recommendations is a direct revenue lever.

**02 — Accelerate Digital Shelf Label Deployment and In-Store AI Inventory Visibility**
Walmart's commitment to deploying DSLs across 5,200 stores by 2027 will reset the industry expectation for price accuracy, real-time inventory signalling, and operational efficiency. IKEA's large-format stores carry significant operational complexity in inventory management across showroom, self-serve, and pick-up areas. A phased DSL rollout, combined with computer-vision-enabled inventory tracking (modelled on Walmart's one-million-associate handheld deployment), would reduce stock discrepancies, enable dynamic pricing for clearance items, and provide real-time fulfilment visibility — directly improving the IKEA in-store conversion rate and customer satisfaction scores.

**03 — Build an Agentic AI Supply Chain Resilience Capability Against Tariff Disruption**
The 2026 tariff environment — including a 25% tariff on AI chip imports and US tariff volatility rated at a 97% geopolitical threat level by Everstream Analytics — is reshaping sourcing and logistics for all global retailers. IKEA's deep dependence on multi-country manufacturing makes this an urgent priority. Following Walmart's Symbotic automation model and Amazon's Project Eluna approach, IKEA should deploy agentic AI supply chain monitoring tools capable of simulating tariff exposure across the full bill of materials, dynamically re-routing inventory, and identifying alternative suppliers in real time. Gartner projects that 60% of supply chain disruptions will be resolved without human involvement by 2031; IKEA must position its supply chain capabilities for that future now.

**04 — Pilot AI-Powered Live Commerce for IKEA Home Styling**
Douyin's live commerce model — ¥7 trillion projected by 2026, luxury sales up 40% YoY — offers a proven template for transforming product discovery into immediate purchase. JD's digital human platform cuts live-streaming costs by 90%, making AI-generated hosts commercially viable. IKEA should pilot AI-assisted live-streaming formats on Douyin (China) and TikTok Shop (Europe/Southeast Asia), featuring AI-generated home styling scenarios, room configuration demonstrations, and real-time purchase links. The "products finding users" model — surfacing IKEA solutions during lifestyle content, not just search — directly aligns with IKEA's aspiration to be present in customers' daily home inspiration journey, not just transactional moments.

**05 — Invest in AI Talent Commensurate with Technology Deployment**
A consistent finding across industry research (KPMG, Deloitte, ISCOM) is that AI investment is outpacing data readiness and human capability: 51% of supply chain leaders cite data accuracy as the biggest AI barrier, while only 42% have real-time supply chain visibility. IKEA should establish an internal AI Fluency Programme for store managers, supply chain planners, and commercial teams — ensuring that when AI tools are deployed, teams can critically interpret outputs, identify model errors, and translate AI recommendations into operational decisions. Technology without AI-literate people will deliver lower returns than the investment justifies; this is a risk already manifesting across the industry.

## e) Risks and Observations

**Risk 1 — US Tariff Escalation on AI Hardware (HIGH)**
The Trump administration announced a 25% tariff on certain AI chip imports in early April 2026, compounding earlier trade pressures. For retailers investing in AI infrastructure — including IKEA's supply chain and in-store digitalization initiatives — this increases the cost of AI hardware deployment and creates timeline risk for automation programmes dependent on imported components. IKEA's global sourcing model and European manufacturing footprint provide partial insulation, but procurement of AI-specific hardware (GPUs, edge compute devices, automation controllers) should be reviewed for tariff exposure and alternative sourcing options identified.

**Risk 2 — AI Investment Outpacing Data Infrastructure Readiness (MEDIUM)**
Industry-wide surveys (Impinj Supply Chain Integrity Outlook 2026; Supply Chain Dive, Jan 2026) indicate that 68% of supply chain leaders plan AI investment, but 51% cite data accuracy as the biggest barrier and only 42% have real-time visibility today. IKEA's own data architecture — spanning 450+ stores, multi-country manufacturing, and fragmented digital channels — may face similar constraints. Without high-quality, real-time data foundations, agentic AI systems will make decisions on incomplete information, producing suboptimal outcomes that erode executive trust in the technology. A data readiness audit should precede any agentic AI deployment.

**Risk 3 — TikTok/ByteDance Regulatory and Governance Uncertainty (WATCH)**
TikTok is consolidating global operations under ByteDance's Beijing leadership amid ongoing US ownership negotiations. Advertisers are pausing or shifting spend due to uncertainty over algorithmic governance and data policies. For IKEA's live commerce and social commerce strategies involving TikTok Shop (particularly in Europe), this governance instability — including over $1 billion reserved for EU regulatory fines and ongoing Digital Services Act probes — creates platform risk. IKEA should maintain channel diversity rather than over-indexing on TikTok for commerce revenue.

**Risk 4 — Conversational Commerce Disintermediation (MEDIUM)**
As Walmart, Sephora, and Amazon integrate with external AI agents (ChatGPT, Gemini), the risk of customer relationship disintermediation grows for retailers who delay. If AI agents become the primary shopping interface, and IKEA is not integrated into those flows, a customer asking an AI assistant for bedroom furniture recommendations will default to brands that are already connected — regardless of IKEA's assortment superiority. This is not a future risk; it is a present-tense competitive vulnerability that requires immediate commercial and technical response.
