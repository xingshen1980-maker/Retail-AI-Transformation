## c) In-Depth Case Analysis

### Case 1 — Walmart's Digital Shelf Label National Rollout: In-Store Digitalization at Hyperscale

**Case Highlights:** Walmart is deploying digital shelf labels (DSLs) across all 4,600+ U.S. stores by end of 2026 under a ~€1 billion contract with VusionGroup (EdgeSense + VusionCloud platforms). Currently live in approximately 2,300 locations, the labels replace paper tags with wirelessly-updated e-ink displays managed centrally via cloud infrastructure. The rollout represents one of the most ambitious in-store infrastructure projects in global retail history.

**Technical Approach:** DSLs are connected to a centralized system enabling headquarters or store managers to push real-time price and promotional updates across tens of thousands of SKUs in seconds. "Pick to light" LED functions guide associates directly to products for online order fulfillment; "Stock to light" functions flag replenishment needs. The system integrates with Walmart's omnichannel operations — including Spark delivery — and operates as a closed system, collecting no customer data. VusionCloud provides the SaaS management layer across all 4,600 stores.

**Business Value:** Store associates report up to 75% reduction in pricing task time, freeing labor for higher-value customer interactions. The system eliminates price discrepancies between shelf and checkout, reduces markdown errors, and accelerates promotional execution. One Walmart electronics team lead described the labels as cutting her pricing duties by three-quarters. However, legislative risk is material: Senators Merkley and Luján have introduced the Stop Price Gouging in Grocery Stores Act, which would ban DSLs in stores over 10,000 sq ft, citing dynamic pricing concerns. Walmart has publicly committed to uniform pricing and is engaging legislators — but consumer and regulatory trust will require sustained proactive communication.

### Case 2 — Amazon Project Kobe: AI-Orchestrated Physical-Digital Retail Fusion

**Case Highlights:** Amazon is developing "Project Kobe" — a new hybrid retail format combining a ~225,000 sq ft supercenter with an embedded robotics-powered fulfillment center. First location confirmed in Orland Park, IL (near Chicago); additional sites in Cherry Hill and Edison, NJ, and Oak Brook, IL; internal documents reference plans for dozens more if early pilots succeed. The format is Amazon's most ambitious physical retail bet to date, targeting Walmart's dominant position in U.S. grocery and general merchandise.

**Technical Approach:** AutoStore robotic grid systems occupy approximately 100,000 sq ft of each store, handling storage, picking, and packaging for in-store, pickup, and delivery orders from a single building. AI assistant "Frida" enables category managers to input strategic goals while models generate inventory recommendations based on demand and space constraints, reducing manual planning at scale. A future proprietary system ("Orbital") is under development for multi-temperature automated handling, but remains approximately two years from launch. The first Kobe stores are expected to open in late 2027.

**Business Value:** Each Kobe store will carry ~250,000 SKUs — nearly double a typical Walmart supercenter — positioning Amazon to aggressively challenge Walmart's 21% U.S. grocery market share from its current 3%. However, fulfillment costs per item are projected 12% higher than Amazon's existing sub-same-day network, reflecting the complexity of integrating fresh grocery with general merchandise automation. Amazon's parallel decision to cancel its "Blue Jay" robotics project after less than six months (February 2026) demonstrates that even at Amazon's scale, AI robotics R&D requires rigorous ROI discipline.

### Case 3 — Sephora × OpenAI: Agentic Commerce and the New Discovery Layer

**Case Highlights:** Sephora launched its app within ChatGPT in March 2026, enabling U.S. customers to discover beauty products, receive personalized advice, and access Beauty Insider loyalty benefits entirely within the ChatGPT interface. Direct checkout functionality is planned for future updates. At Shoptalk Spring 2026, Sephora's global CDO framed the priority as ensuring the brand remains a trusted adviser regardless of channel.

**Technical Approach:** The Sephora ChatGPT app accepts natural-language beauty queries and surfaces curated recommendations informed by the user's Beauty Insider loyalty profile when linked. OpenAI's platform provides the conversational layer; Sephora provides the domain expertise, product catalog, and loyalty context. The initiative positions Sephora at the moment of consumer intent within a channel that OpenAI describes as "increasingly the starting point for how people discover products and make decisions." A new sponsored experiences model within ChatGPT creates a retail media layer where brand visibility is earned through relevance, not just purchased through targeting.

**Business Value:** Sephora's ChatGPT integration establishes an AI-native commerce channel ahead of competitors, embedding brand authority into conversational discovery at scale. The move is part of LVMH-owned Sephora's 2026 digital innovation agenda, which simultaneously advances AI demand forecasting (integrating influencer signals, social sentiment, and weather into dynamic inventory decisions), omnichannel UK expansion (20 stores this year), and digital twin supply chain modeling. Competitor activity — Glossier, Skims, and Target have also launched ChatGPT storefronts — confirms the channel is approaching commercial mainstream.

## d) Insights and Recommendations for IKEA Business

**01 — Enter AI Shopping Agent Channels Before Visibility Gaps Widen**
Sephora's ChatGPT integration and Walmart's Gemini partnership establish that conversational AI platforms are becoming primary product discovery channels. Home furnishing — inherently consultative and inspiration-driven — is well-suited to this format. IKEA should pilot integration with at least one major AI shopping assistant (ChatGPT or Google Gemini) for high-frequency categories such as storage, lighting, and textiles. Critical prerequisite: ensure IKEA's product catalog is structured for AI readability — clean taxonomy, standardized attributes, real-time inventory APIs — to appear in organic AI recommendations.

**02 — Evaluate Digital Shelf Label Deployment Across IKEA's Large-Format Estate**
Walmart's national DSL rollout establishes a new competitive baseline for large-format in-store operations. IKEA's stores, carrying tens of thousands of SKUs across enormous physical footprints, would derive measurable labor savings and pricing accuracy benefits from DSL deployment. Commission a feasibility study across 5–10 pilot stores, defining success metrics for associate time savings, pricing accuracy, and omnichannel fulfillment speed. Engage VusionGroup or equivalent suppliers early, and develop consumer trust communications proactively given the legislative climate around dynamic pricing.

**03 — Transform IKEA's Design Expertise into an AI-Native Commerce Asset**
Unlike grocery or beauty, IKEA's core proposition is home transformation — a high-consideration, inspiration-driven journey. Generative AI and real-time 3D visualization (validated by Wonder Suite's 3D Cloud integration at RTS 2026) offer a unique opportunity to build room-planning AI agents that recommend complete room solutions from IKEA's catalog. This would convert IKEA's unparalleled design expertise into a scalable AI capability that commodity retailers cannot replicate, creating a structural competitive moat.

**04 — Prioritize AI-Driven Supply Chain Resilience Against Trade Cost Pressures**
Deloitte's 2026 Retail Global Outlook confirms 95% of retail executives anticipate rising costs due to global trade policy volatility, with 66% planning supply chain restructuring. Only 30% of retailers currently employ AI for supply chain visibility, with the figure expected to reach 41% within 12 months. IKEA, with its globally distributed sourcing network, should accelerate AI-powered demand forecasting and dynamic inventory rebalancing — particularly for import-dependent product lines — to protect margins against tariff-driven cost shocks and logistics disruption.

**05 — Build AI Workforce Readiness Before Deployments Scale**
Walmart's commitment to training all 1.6 million associates via OpenAI certification before its AI tools fully scale reflects a critical principle: workforce readiness must precede technology rollout. IKEA should develop a structured AI literacy and co-pilot training program for store associates, supply chain staff, and digital teams — establishing internal champions who can accelerate adoption and surface use cases specific to IKEA's operating model, culture, and customer relationships.

## e) Risks and Observations

**Dynamic Pricing Legislative Risk (HIGH)**
Walmart's DSL rollout has triggered U.S. Senate legislation proposing to ban digital shelf labels in large-format grocery stores. This risk reflects genuine consumer anxiety about AI-enabled pricing opacity. Retailers deploying digitally-enabled pricing infrastructure must develop proactive consumer communication strategies and engage regulators early. IKEA should monitor EU regulatory developments on algorithmic and dynamic pricing as a leading indicator for potential European legislation affecting in-store digital systems.

**Agentic Commerce Channel Fragmentation (MEDIUM)**
The strategic divergence between Walmart's open AI ecosystem (Gemini, OpenAI, Universal Commerce Protocol) and Amazon's closed approach (Rufus, Buy for Me) creates fragmentation risk for retailers not integrated into both surfaces. Retailers absent from AI-mediated discovery risk disappearing at the moment of consumer intent. IKEA must evaluate multi-platform participation — not reliance on a single AI commerce channel — to avoid structural visibility gaps as agentic commerce scales.

**AI R&D ROI Discipline Imperative (WATCH)**
Amazon's swift cancellation of the Blue Jay robotics project after less than six months signals that even the most AI-sophisticated organizations are imposing ruthless ROI discipline on experimental initiatives. As IKEA's AI investments expand, robust evaluation frameworks with explicit success metrics, defined timelines, and stage-gate reviews are essential to prevent sunk-cost escalation and maintain strategic focus.

**Data Infrastructure as the Binding Constraint (MEDIUM)**
Cloudera's April 2026 global survey of 1,300 IT leaders found that nearly 80% of enterprises report AI initiatives are constrained by data access limitations — not algorithms. For IKEA, fragmented data across store POS systems, e-commerce platforms, CRM, loyalty programs, and supply chain tools represents the most significant barrier to AI ROI. A unified, enterprise-wide data foundation must be treated as a strategic priority, not a back-office IT project, to unlock the full potential of AI investment.
